Gestational diabetes is a type of diabetes that develops during pregnancy in women who did not already have diabetes. Every year, many expecting mothers in Bodinayakanur, Sillamarathupatti, and surrounding regions in Theni are diagnosed with this condition.

At **Vyasini Multi Specialty Hospital**, we prioritize maternal care and work closely with pregnant women to manage their blood sugar levels, ensuring a safe pregnancy and a healthy delivery.

## What Causes Gestational Diabetes?

During pregnancy, the placenta produces hormones that help the baby grow. However, these hormones can sometimes block the action of the mother's insulin, leading to insulin resistance. When the body cannot produce enough insulin to overcome this resistance, glucose levels rise, resulting in gestational diabetes.

## Key Risk Factors

While any pregnant woman can develop gestational diabetes, some factors increase the risk:
- Being overweight or obese before pregnancy.
- Having a family history of diabetes.
- Having gestational diabetes in a previous pregnancy.
- Being over the age of 25.

## Symptoms of Gestational Diabetes

Most women with gestational diabetes do not experience active or noticeable symptoms. It is usually detected during routine prenatal screenings. However, some mild symptoms include:
- Increased thirst
- More frequent urination
- Fatigue
- Blurred vision

## How We Screen and Diagnose

At Vyasini Hospital, we conduct standard glucose screening tests for all pregnant women between **24 and 28 weeks** of pregnancy. If you have higher risk factors, screening may be recommended earlier.

## Tips for Managing Gestational Diabetes

1. **Follow a Tailored Meal Plan**: Focus on complex carbohydrates (like brown rice, millets, oats), lean proteins, and plenty of fiber-rich vegetables. Avoid refined sugars and processed foods.
2. **Engage in Regular Physical Activity**: Safe exercises like brisk walking for 30 minutes a day can help lower blood sugar levels and improve insulin sensitivity.
3. **Monitor Blood Sugar Levels**: You may need to check your blood glucose levels several times a day using a home glucometer.
4. **Regular Medical Checkups**: Keep up with all prenatal visits to monitor your baby's growth and discuss blood sugar trends with our medical team.

If lifestyle changes are not sufficient to manage blood glucose, our specialists may recommend safe oral medications or insulin therapy.
