Accidents and medical emergencies can strike at any time. Knowing what to do in the first few minutes can make a massive difference in patient recovery. 

As the premier **24/7 Emergency Care Hospital in Bodinayakanur & Sillamarathupatti**, the trauma team at **Vyasini Multi Specialty Hospital** shares these essential first-aid guidelines for families.

## 1. Dealing with Minor to Severe Cuts and Bleeding

- **Apply Direct Pressure**: Use a clean cloth, sterile gauze, or your hand to apply firm, direct pressure to the wound to stop bleeding.
- **Elevate the Injury**: If possible, raise the bleeding limb above the level of the heart.
- **Clean Gently**: For minor cuts, wash with clean running water. Avoid putting strong chemicals or home remedies on open wounds.
- **Do Not Remove Embedded Objects**: If an object is deeply embedded, do not pull it out. Secure it in place and head to our emergency room.

## 2. Managing Burns

- **Cool the Burn**: Place the burned area under cool running tap water for at least 10 to 15 minutes. Do not use ice, as extreme cold can damage the tissue further.
- **Cover Loosely**: Cover the burn with a clean, dry, non-stick dressing.
- **Avoid Popping Blisters**: Blisters act as a natural sterile barrier. Popping them increases the risk of infection.
- **Seek Emergency Care**: If the burn is large, on the face, hands, or joints, or caused by chemicals/electricity, seek immediate medical care.

## 3. Suspected Bone Fractures

- **Immobilize the Area**: Keep the injured limb as still as possible. Do not try to re-align the bone.
- **Apply Ice Packs**: Wrap ice in a towel and apply to the area to reduce swelling.
- **Control Bleeding**: If there is an open wound with the fracture, apply pressure to stop bleeding, but avoid pressing directly on the bone.

## 4. Recognizing Heart Attacks and Administering CPR

If a person collapses, becomes unresponsive, and is not breathing normally:
- **Call for Help**: Immediately call Vyasini Hospital's Emergency Line: **+91 99440 61805** or arrange transport.
- **Start Chest Compressions (CPR)**: Place your hands in the center of the person's chest. Press down hard and fast at a rate of 100 to 120 compressions per minute (to the beat of the song "Stayin' Alive").
- **Continue Compressions**: Keep going until medical help arrives or the person shows signs of breathing/movement.
