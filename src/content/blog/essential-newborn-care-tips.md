Bringing a newborn home is one of the happiest moments in a family's life. However, it also brings a lot of questions, especially for first-time parents in Sillamarathupatti, Bodinayakanur, and nearby towns.

To support you, the **Paediatrics & Child Care Department at Vyasini Multi Specialty Hospital** has put together this comprehensive guide on essential newborn care.

## 1. Feeding and Nutrition

Breast milk is the ideal food for newborns, providing all necessary nutrients and antibodies to build immunity.
- **On-Demand Feeding**: Feed your newborn whenever they show signs of hunger (rooting, sucking hands, crying). This usually translates to feeding every 2 to 3 hours.
- **Burping the Baby**: Always burp your baby after feeds to release swallowed air and reduce spitting up or colic.

## 2. Sleeping Safety

Newborns sleep up to 16 to 18 hours a day, but usually in short blocks of 2 to 4 hours.
- **Back to Sleep**: Always place your baby on their back to sleep on a firm, flat surface.
- **Clear Crib**: Keep the crib free of loose blankets, pillows, stuffed toys, or bumpers to prevent accidental suffocation.

## 3. Hygiene and Cord Care

- **Sponge Baths**: Stick to gentle sponge baths until the umbilical cord stump falls off (usually 1 to 3 weeks).
- **Keep it Dry**: Keep the umbilical stump clean and dry. Avoid covering it with diapers; fold the diaper down to expose the stump to air.
- **Diapering**: Change diapers frequently to prevent diaper rash. Clean the area gently with warm water and soft wipes, and apply a barrier cream if needed.

## 4. Understanding Immunization

Vaccinating your child is the most effective way to protect them from dangerous childhood diseases.
- **BCG, Polio, and Hepatitis B**: These are typically given at birth or within the first few days.
- **Follow-up Schedules**: Our child care specialists will provide a customized immunization card detailing all recommended vaccines up to 5+ years.

## When to Consult a Doctor Immediately

Watch out for these warning signs in your newborn and consult a paediatrician immediately:
- A rectal temperature of 100.4°F (38°C) or higher.
- Poor feeding or refusal to breastfeed.
- Persistent vomiting or watery stools.
- Difficulty breathing or rapid breathing.
- Extreme lethargy or constant, high-pitched crying.
